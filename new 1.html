To the untrained eye, a photo is a photo. To an automotive photographer, each image is unique in its form and format. Every click of the shutter offers the opportunity to perfectly capture a vehicleâ€™s purpose and personality.

The art of automotive photography itself is a unique field. It requires a specialized set of skills and knowledge, and offers its own challenges that differ from other types of advertising photography. It is far more than simply pointing a camera at a car and hoping for a great shot.

With automotive photography, the car is the focal point, not models. This changes many aspects of the photographer's techniques as well as the requirements for a good image. When focusing on a car, the photographer must interact with an inanimate object to make it come alive in the image. They must maintain the focus of. the image on the car rather than other objects in the photo. The color of the car is key in this focus. Just as models should wear colors that are flattering for their skin tones, a car must wear the right lighting and background to enhance its color. Some car colors work well in direct sunlight, while others do poorly. Additionally, selecting a background that includes a highlight of the car's color can bring out the beauty of the paint job.
